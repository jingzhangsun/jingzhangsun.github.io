SMILab develops trustworthy artificial intelligence methods for quantitative nuclear medicine and medical image analysis. We aim to translate AI-based imaging methods into robust, interpretable, and scalable tools for nuclear medicine practice.

<strong>Low-count myocardial perfusion SPECT imaging methods</strong>    
The overall goal is to improve myocardial perfusion SPECT imaging under reduced-dose, shortened-acquisition, and cardiac-gated conditions.  
- Develop deep learning methods for low-dose myocardial perfusion SPECT denoising and quantitative recovery.[(Sun et al. QIMS 2022)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9246746/) [(Sun et al. J. Nucl. Cardiol. 2023)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12350-022-03045-x)  
- Develop generalized diffusion models for efficient cardiac-gated MP-SPECT image restoration.[(Li et al. EJNMMI Phy. 2026)](https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1186/s40658-025-00825-5.pdf)  
- Investigate the patient-specific learning strategies for robust SPECT quantification.[(Sun et al. Front. Med. 2023)](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/medicine/articles/10.3389/fmed.2023.1083413/full) [(Dong et al. IEEE NSS/MIC 2025)](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11287664)  

<strong>Motion-aware cardiac-gated SPECT restoration</strong>   
The goal aims to reduce noise, motion artifacts, deblurring effects, and temporal inconsistency in cardiac-gated myocardial perfusion SPECT.  
- Develop dual-gated and cardiac-gated SPECT denoising methods.[(Sun et al. Med. Phys. 2022)]() [(Sun et al. IEEE NSS/MIC 2019)]()  
- Develop diffusion-based models for simultaneous denoising, deblurring, and motion-artifact reduction.[(Li et al. IEEE NSS/MIC 2025)]() [(Liu et al. IEEE TRPMS 2026)](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/11489069)  
- Explore resource-efficient and distributed learning frameworks for cardiac-gated SPECT restoration.(Li et al. IEEE NSS/MIC 2026)
